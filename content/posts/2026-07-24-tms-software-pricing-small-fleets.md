## What TMS Software Actually Costs Small Fleets: Breaking Down the Real Numbers

When you're running a small fleet of 10 to 75 power units, every dollar matters. We've talked to hundreds of dispatchers and fleet managers who got burned by TMS pricing that looked reasonable upfront but ballooned as their operations grew. The truth about TMS pricing isn't pretty – and it's time someone laid out the real costs.

### The Hidden Trap of Per-User Pricing Models

Most TMS providers use per-user pricing that starts around $50-150 per month per user. Sounds reasonable until you do the math on what "user" actually means in your operation.

Your dispatcher needs access. So does your safety manager. The bookkeeper handling settlements and invoicing needs a login. Your maintenance coordinator tracking vehicle inspections requires access. The operations manager overseeing everything needs full visibility. That owner-operator you just brought on board needs to update his location and submit PODs.

What looked like $100/month for one user quickly becomes $500-800/month for a basic setup. Add seasonal drivers, part-time dispatchers, or grow from 15 trucks to 30 trucks, and you're looking at $1,200+ monthly just for software access.

We built Techvia TMS because this pricing model punishes growth – the exact opposite of what small fleets need.

### Real TMS Costs Beyond the Base Price

The sticker price rarely tells the whole story. Here's what dispatchers we work with discovered after signing contracts:

**Implementation and Setup Fees**: Many providers charge $2,000-5,000 upfront for "onboarding" and data migration. This often involves weeks of back-and-forth, training sessions that don't stick, and frustrating delays getting your operation live.

**Integration Costs**: Your TMS needs to talk to your fuel cards, ELD systems, accounting software, and load boards. Each integration often carries monthly fees ranging from $25-100 per connection. Connect to three systems and you've added $300/month to your bill.

**Training and Support**: Premium support packages run $200-500/month extra. Without them, you're stuck with basic email support that takes days to resolve dispatch-critical issues. When a driver's sitting with detention time stacking up, slow support costs real money.

**Contract Lock-in**: Annual contracts with early termination fees create another hidden cost. Change your mind after six months and face penalties of 50-75% of remaining contract value.

### Why Small Fleets Get Hit Hardest

Large carriers can absorb TMS costs because they spread them across hundreds of units. When you're running 25 trucks, that $2,000 monthly TMS bill represents $80 per truck per month – a significant chunk of your per-mile profit margin.

The per-user model especially hurts growing operations. You start with two users and manageable costs. Six months later, you've added drivers who need mobile access, brought on a part-time dispatcher for weekend coverage, and your mechanic needs system access for maintenance scheduling. Your TMS bill doubled while your truck count only grew 20%.

This pricing structure forces small fleets into impossible choices: limit system access and hurt operational efficiency, or pay enterprise prices for small fleet operations.

### Making the TMS Investment Decision

Calculate total cost of ownership over 24 months, not just monthly fees. Include implementation, training, integrations, support, and projected user growth. A TMS that costs $300/month but requires $5,000 setup plus $200/month for each additional user will cost more than a $500/month flat-rate system over two years of normal growth.

Consider operational efficiency gains too. The right TMS should reduce detention claims through better tracking, speed up settlements, improve customer communication, and eliminate manual paperwork. These efficiency improvements often save more than the software costs.

Factor in switching costs as well. Changing TMS systems means data migration, retraining staff, updating customer processes, and potential operational disruptions. Getting the decision right the first time matters more than saving $100/month upfront.
